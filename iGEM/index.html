Our Project

Hong Kong has had a thriving oyster industry for 700 years. The presence of oysters in Hong Kong’s bays and oceans has helped the livelihoods of thousands of fishermen and grown the seafood industry that is so ingrained in Hong Kong’s vibrant aquaculture. However, one aspect of this key industry has not been addressed. Oyster contamination.

Oysters are often contaminated with bacteria vibrio spp., a common gram-negative bacteria often found in marine environments. Contamination takes place as oysters feed. When filtering water, this bacteria can often become concentrated in oyster tissue, making it dangerous for consumption, especially raw.
